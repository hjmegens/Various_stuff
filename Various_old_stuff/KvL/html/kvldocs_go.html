Search Option: Gene Ontology (GO)

This option allows you to retrieve all the genes associated with a GO term or GO id of choice. The input, i.e. term or id, need to be exact matches. In case a term is submitted that has no exact matches an attempt is made to provide suggestions, but this feature is currently not very effective.

When a valid term (or id) is provided, all the genes that are associated with it or its child terms are retrieved. A list of child terms will be provided, as will be a list of parent terms for reference. All parent-child relationships are retrieved from a local database that is based directly on the Gene Ontology database. Associations are based solely on human since this species has the best GO annotation, and species specific genes are subsequently obtained by orthologous relationships (EnsEMBL compara). GO annotations are from a local databased but were mined from NCBI, that in turn retrieves this information from the Gene Ontology database. Local databases are updated twice a year.

What to expect:

Let's assume you have entered a valid GO term or id.

It may take some time for results to appear. Then you will see:


GO term 'GO:0040007 - growth' is parent of the following terms:

Dist. GO acc  GO name
0 GO:0040007 growth
1 GO:0016049 cell growth
1 GO:0035264 multicellular organism growth

etc


GO term 'GO:0040007 - growth' is descendant of the following terms:

Dist. GO acc  GO name
0 GO:0040007 growth
1 GO:0008150 biological_process
2 all all

Dist. refers to the distance between parent and child terms in the ontology tree.

Subsequently, a list of human gene names will be displayed, and finally, for each of the gene names, a description will be given and the species specific gene by finding the ortholog, e.g.:


HGNC symbol BMPR1B has approved name bone morphogenetic protein receptor, type IB - chicken ortholog (1-1) is ENSGALG00000012216


If the search is complete, you will notice a button at the bottom of the page stating 'submit'. If you press this button, the gene(s) that were found can be process in the SNPsearch phase. Alternatively, you can make a selection of your genes and submit.


If you did not submit a valid term or id you will be notified about it and you can choose to start a new query by pressing 'new'.
